    Choosing your stock

    The stock is the single biggest driver of how a poster looks and feels in the room. Here's how we'd guide you:

    Satin photo

    The all-rounder, and what 8 in 10 customers pick. Deep colour and a glare-free surface that reads cleanly under booth lighting. Our default recommendation for event posters.

    Gloss

    Maximum shine and colour pop — fantastic for photography and high-contrast art, though it can show glare under direct spotlights.

    Matte

    A flat, non-reflective finish that's easy to read from any angle. The safe choice for text-heavy posters and anything near a window.

    Canvas

    A textured, fine-art surface for pieces meant to live on a wall well past the show. Pairs beautifully with a mounted finish.

    Sizes & orientation

    24 × 36″ is the standard event poster and our most-ordered size — large enough to anchor a booth, small enough to ship and handle easily. Step up to 27 × 40″ (the classic movie one-sheet) or 36 × 48″ for a backdrop-scale statement, or down to 18 × 24″ for tabletop signs and easel displays. Need something specific? Enter any custom size up to 120″ on a side. Flip between portrait and landscape at the top of the builder — the proof and price update instantly.

    Finishing

    A laminate (gloss or matte) adds a protective layer that guards against scuffs, fingerprints and moisture, and lets a poster be wiped clean — worth it for anything handled repeatedly or used show after show. Gloss laminate deepens colour and shine; matte laminate kills glare and gives a smooth, premium hand-feel.

    How to set up your file

    • Resolution: aim for 300 dpi at final print size. We accept down to 150 dpi but will flag anything that will look soft — and the live proof shows you exactly how it'll read.
    • Bleed: include 1/8″ (0.125″) of bleed on every edge so there's no white sliver after trimming.
    • Safe zone: keep important text and logos at least 1/8″ inside the trim so nothing gets cut.
    • Colour: supply CMYK where you can; we'll convert RGB and let you know if anything shifts.
    • Fonts & formats: a print-ready PDF with fonts embedded is ideal. AI, EPS, PSD, TIFF, PNG and JPG are all welcome.

    Not sure your file is right? Upload it anyway — every order is preflighted automatically the moment it lands, then a human proofs it before it prints. You'll see the catch (and the one-click fix) right on the poster above.

    Turnaround & same-day at the MTCC

    Standard production is 5 business days, with rush, next-day and same-day options surfaced live as you pick your in-hand date. If you're exhibiting at the Metro Toronto Convention Centre, order before the daily cut-off and pick up the same day from the Business Centre on the North (300 Level) or South (800 Level) buildings — or have it couriered to your booth. There's no weekend production, so the builder bakes weekends and cut-offs into the date you're shown.

    Frequently asked questions

    What's the cheapest way to print a 24 × 36 poster?

    Satin photo with no laminate is the best value, and the per-poster price drops on multiples — ordering 5+ saves about 10%, and 25+ saves about 25%. Giving yourself more lead time also lowers the price; the calendar shows exactly how much you save at each in-hand date.

    Can I get a poster printed today in Toronto?

    Yes — same-day printing is available for orders placed before the daily cut-off, with pickup at the MTCC or courier delivery across the GTA. Pick a same-day in-hand date in the builder and it'll confirm whether you've made the cut-off.

    What resolution do I need?

    300 dpi at final size is ideal; 150 dpi is the practical minimum. The live proof flags low-resolution files and offers a one-click upscale so you can see the difference before you commit.

    Do you proof my file before printing?

    Always. Every order is auto-preflighted for resolution, bleed and safe-zone the moment it's uploaded, and a person reviews it before it goes to press. Free on every order.

    Should I add a laminate?

    If the poster will be handled, transported between shows, or hung anywhere it might get touched or splashed — yes. Laminate lets it be wiped clean and roughly doubles its working life. For a one-night wall piece, the bare stock is fine.

    Can you match a specific colour?

    We print CMYK and can advise on the closest match. For critical brand colours, send a reference and we'll confirm before the run.
